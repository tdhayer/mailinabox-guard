Spam & Filtering

Configure how your mail server handles spam and unwanted mail. Changes take effect immediately after saving.

SpamAssassin Spam Threshold

SpamAssassin scores every incoming email. Messages scoring above the threshold are marked as spam and moved to the Spam folder. A lower threshold is more aggressive (catches more spam but may have false positives).

5.0 Normal (recommended)
1 — Very Aggressive 5 — Normal 10 — Very Lenient

Greylisting

Greylisting temporarily rejects mail from unknown senders. Legitimate servers retry delivery after a short delay, but most spam sources do not. This is very effective at reducing spam but causes a small delivery delay for first-time senders.

Warning: Greylisting is disabled. Your server will accept all incoming mail without the initial reject-and-retry check. This may result in significantly more spam.

How long (in seconds) a new sender must wait before their mail is accepted. Default is 180 seconds (3 minutes). Lower values reduce the delay but may let more spam through.

Spamhaus Data Query Service (DQS)

Mail-in-a-Box uses Spamhaus DNS blocklists to filter spam. Public DNS resolvers (like Cloudflare's 1.1.1.1 or Google's 8.8.8.8) are blocked by Spamhaus. If your server queries Spamhaus through a public resolver, large amounts of spam will bypass the filter.

You can create a free Spamhaus DQS account and enter your API Key below to securely query Spamhaus and bypass public resolver blocks.

This key will be automatically configured in Postfix to reject spam at the network level.
